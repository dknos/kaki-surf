## Framing

Keep the complete 16:9 gameplay frame visible inside the vertical master whenever the hero barrel is on screen. Fill the unused upper and lower space with a color-matched, softened duplicate of the live frame rather than cropping away the lip, aperture, contact point, or Kaki. Controlled punch-ins may reach roughly 125% for landings and animal beats, but every crop must preserve the action's telegraph and result.

Use only two editorial text beats: the opening challenge and the final call to action. In-game trick, landing, combo, and result text carries the middle. Never stack editorial copy over a gameplay callout, the rider, the pitching lip, or the likely landing zone.

## Capture and edit rules

- Capture actual runtime motion at release settings. The deterministic QA scenes are storyboards and continuity checks, not substitute animation frames.
- Keep world energy moving consistently through each cut. Do not mirror footage to fake direction; the wave texture and distant water must never reverse with the rider.
- Use action-matched cuts: carve apex, pump release, lip takeoff, completed rotation, board contact, animal telegraph, and collapse impact.
- Let every new beat change at least one clear dimension: speed, wave scale, airtime, risk, interaction, or payoff.
- Prefer the game's live water, wind, landing, wildlife, combo, and threat mix. If music is replaced, use cleared material and keep gameplay impacts audible.
- Do not speed-ramp through telegraphs or landings. Intensity must come from the real run, framing, sound, and escalation.
- Validate the final export on a phone at normal playback size. Kaki, board direction, lip, landing area, score result, and both editorial overlays must remain legible.

## Acceptance

The opening frame matches the title's promise; the hero barrel is the largest and longest-held image; the line from downhill speed to launch to landing is understandable without narration; no shot contains a stray boat, reversible horizontal line field, atlas seam, overlapping callout, or misleading fake action; and the ending shows an earned result before cutting cleanly.
